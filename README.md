# SPCE 5025 - Fundamentals of Astronautics

**University of Colorado Colorado Springs | Spring 2026**

## Course Description

Introduces the fundamental principles of astrodynamics applied to satellite motion. Includes orbital mechanics, coordinate systems, two-body problems, orbit determination, and orbital maneuvers.

**Instructor:** Edward Brown
**Prerequisite:** SPCE 5005 - Engineering Analysis for Space Applications

## Topics Covered

- Two-Body Problem & Kepler's Equation
- Orbit State Representations
- Vectors, Coordinate Systems & Transformations
- Numerical Integration Techniques (Runge-Kutta, Multi-step methods)
- Sun/Moon Computations & Time Systems
- Perturbations (Gravity, drag, 3rd-body, solar pressure)
- Satellite Observations (Range, azimuth, elevation)
- Orbit Estimation & Determination (Least squares, Kalman filters)
- Orbital Maneuvers & Targeting
- Mission Orbits & Analysis

## Required Texts

- *Fundamentals of Astrodynamics and Applications*, David A. Vallado, 5th Edition (2022)
- *Fundamentals of Astrodynamics*, Bate, Mueller, White (1971)
